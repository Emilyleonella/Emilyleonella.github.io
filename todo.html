Galapagos Islands

The Galapgos is a group of islands in the Pacific ocean. The islands are known for its diversity of plants and wildlife. One of the most interesting animals you can find is the galapagos giant tortise. These tortise are the worlds largest tortise that can weigh an average of 400lbs, and can live an average of more than hundreds of years. This is a very popular place to visit, due to the wildlife being very freindly since they are use to humans being around. Click here to learn how to visit, Expedition: Galapagos.

Price:Free Ages:Everyone

Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon rainforest spreads across most of South America. Ecuador is blessed to house a part of this beautiful ecosytem. It is home to hundreds of mammals, reptiles, birds, thousands of insects, and tree species. If you love nature and widlife this is a must see when visiting Ecuador. Learn how to tour at rainforest tours.

Palion del Diablo Waterfall

If you love adventure and are a hiking enthusiast,you'll love visiting Palion del Diablo (translated to 'Devil's Cauldron' in English). Palion del Diabo is located in central Ecuador, in a city named Baños. Palion del Diablo's waterfall is the most breath-taking waterfall in the whole country. Aside from the waterfall, Baños, also has lots of thermal pools and hot springs. It is said that these waters have healing powers and are filled with rich minerals. Plan a trip to Baños here.

Price:Free Ages:Everyone

Teleférico de Quito

A Teleférico, is a cable car, these cable cars are located in the capital and it takes you from Quito all the way up to the side of the volcano, Pichincha. It is an amazing view and a gentle ride for everyone in the family. Once you arrive to the top you are able to take the path all the way up the volcano to the peak. The only downside is, it's very cold at the top, but it is well worth the hike. Ways to get to the cable car.

Prices:Under 18: $7.00, Adults: $8.50, Senior/Disable: $6.50 Ages:Everyone
